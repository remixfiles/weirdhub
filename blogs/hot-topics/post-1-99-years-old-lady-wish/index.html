At 99, Her Only Bucket List Wish Was to Be Arrested—And the Police Actually Made It Happen

By Maria Lichi • 6 July, 2026

Back in 2017, a 99-year-old woman named Annie from Nijmegen in the Netherlands had one strange wish on her bucket list. She had always wanted to know what it felt like to get arrested by the police at least once in her life. Annie loved true crime stories and real-life mysteries, so this idea had been stuck in her head for years.

When the local police heard about it from her niece, they didn’t laugh it off. Instead, they decided to make her dream come true in a kind and safe way. They went to her house, gently put handcuffs on her, drove her to the station in a police car, and let her sit inside a holding cell for a few minutes. There were no charges, no real arrest — it was all planned just for her.

Annie had the biggest smile on her face the whole time. Even with the handcuffs on, she looked so happy and satisfied. The police later posted a few photos on their Facebook page, and the story quickly went viral around the world. People loved how thoughtful the officers were. For Annie, it was a lifelong wish finally checked off, and she enjoyed every single second of it.
