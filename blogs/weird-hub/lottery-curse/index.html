From Refuse Collector to Multi-Millionaire and Back Again: The Rise and Fall of Britain's Most Infamous Lottery Winner

By WeirdHub Team • 14 July, 2026

From Refuse Collector to Overnight Millionaire: Carroll's 2002 Jackpot and Wild Lifestyle

In 2002, 19-year-old Michael Carroll, a refuse collector in the United Kingdom, saw his life change overnight after winning £9,736,131 in the National Lottery, roughly 13 million US dollars. The young worker with a prior criminal record quickly spent on a mansion-like home and luxury cars. He threw lavish parties and indulged heavily in cocaine and women. Carroll claimed he spent about 2,000 US dollars daily on cocaine and 1,000 dollars on sex parties while sleeping with more than 4,000 women. British tabloids dubbed him the "King of Chavs" and "Lotto Lout" for his flashy excess. His story became a national sensation, showing the dream of instant wealth and the risks when a young man with no financial experience receives millions. Media coverage was constant as Carroll embraced the fame and spent without restraint, turning his binman past into tabloid legend almost instantly.

Legal Woes, Family Rift and Rapid Financial Ruin

Carroll's reckless spending soon brought consequences. He grew distant from family. He faced legal troubles including drug-related crimes and theft cases, resulting in convictions, plus a 2005 ASBO and nine-month jail term in 2006 for affray. These issues, combined with constant parties and drugs, drained his fortune in just a few years. By 2010 he was completely broke and living on government benefits. The millions vanished on cars, homes, jewellery, parties and substances. Tabloids that once celebrated his lifestyle now documented his public downfall. Carroll's case became a classic example of the "lottery curse," where sudden wealth without planning leads to swift ruin and personal isolation.

Back to Labour: Coal Work and No Regrets

Carroll has repeatedly said he has no regrets about how he spent the money. He applied to return to his old refuse job and did so in 2010. He later moved to coal delivery work in Scotland, often seven days a week. He has stated he feels happier and more grounded in this simple working life than during his millionaire years. Today the former jackpot winner earns his living through manual labour, far from palaces and parties. His full story remains one of the most striking lottery tales in Britain, proving sudden fortune can vanish as fast as it arrives yet leave the person unapologetic about the ride.
